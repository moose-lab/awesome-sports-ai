# Awesome Sports AI: 2026 Strategic Roadmap

## 1. Vision and 2026 Landscape

The sports AI industry is projected to reach nearly $50 billion by 2033, growing at over 21% annually [1]. As we approach the massive FIFA World Cup 2026 in North America, the intersection of artificial intelligence and sports is shifting from enterprise-only platforms to democratized, open-source builder tools. 

Key trends driving this shift include:
* **Generative AI and Multimodal LLMs**: Tools like SportR and video reasoning LLMs are enabling natural language queries over game footage and automated highlight generation.
* **Computer Vision for All**: Open-source projects like Roboflow Sports are democratizing ball tracking, player re-identification, and spatial analytics.
* **Women's Sports Explosion**: The WNBA and women's soccer are seeing unprecedented viewership and investment, creating a massive demand for dedicated analytics and coverage tools.
* **Emerging Sports Analytics**: Fast-growing sports like Pickleball (e.g., PB Vision) and Esports require specialized, lightweight AI coaching and tracking tools.
* **Fan Co-Viewing and Agentic AI**: AI agents (like BleacherBot) are acting as co-viewing partners, while "phygital" venues blend live and digital experiences.

## 2. High-Profile Early-Stage Project Opportunities

To make `awesome-sports-ai` the central hub for this revolution, we are highlighting specific "mono-tool" projects that individual developers can build right now. These map directly to the enterprise capabilities but focus on accessible, open-source solutions.

### 2.1 The "World Cup 2026" AI Suite
* **`llm-match-commentator`**: An open-source RAG system that ingests live match event streams and generates localized, multi-language commentary or tactical summaries.
* **`var-vision-lite`**: A lightweight computer vision tool to calibrate camera angles and detect offside lines from broadcast footage, mimicking enterprise semi-automated offside tech.
* **`fan-sentiment-tracker`**: A social media ingestion tool that uses NLP to gauge real-time fan sentiment during major tournament matches.

### 2.2 The "Women's Sports" Analytics Engine
* **`wnba-gravity-mapper`**: A tool to calculate player "gravity" (how much defensive attention a player draws) using public WNBA play-by-play and tracking data.
* **`nwsl-highlight-clipper`**: An automated tool that uses audio peaks (crowd noise) and visual cues to auto-clip highlights from NWSL or WSL matches.

### 2.3 The "Emerging Sports" Coaching Assistants
* **`pickleball-court-mapper`**: A mobile-friendly CV tool to map pickleball court lines and track ball bounces for amateur match analysis.
* **`esports-action-spotter`**: A tool that analyzes Twitch streams of games like Valorant or League of Legends to tag key events and generate coaching feedback.

## 3. Enhancing the Contributor Funnel

To make the project more welcoming and structured:

1. **Structured GitHub Issues**: We will expand the `.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE` to include specific "Project Ideas" and "Request for Build" templates, encouraging the community to tackle the roadmap items.
2. **"Good First Issue" Tagging**: Maintainers will actively tag decomposed mono-tools (e.g., `team-id-resolver`) as good first issues.
3. **Showcase Directory**: A new section in the README to highlight active, high-quality open-source projects born from this roadmap.
